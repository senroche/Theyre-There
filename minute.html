Students, We are facing an epidemic. Ireland now has one of the highest rates of mental health illness in Europe. ranking joint third out of the 36 countries surveyed. 18.5% of the Irish population was recorded as having a mental health disorder. such as anxiety. bipolar. schizophrenia. depression, alcohol or drug use. in 2016 and unfortunately this figure is only increasing. Within the last year 21% of young adults reported that at some point, they had engaged in deliberate self - harm. This equates to 1 in 5 people, meaning If not you, it could be your friend, sibling, partner or simply just the person sitting next to you, who are all struggling unnecessarily. It is evidently clear that too many people within this country, within our country. are needlessly suffering in silence. something which needs to and can be changed.

This is where the "They're there" campaign makes a difference. This is a campaign orchestrated by students to promote the existing facilities and services which are available to help when individuals are struggling to keep on top of their mental health and wellbeing. Over the next three days you will notice that posters have been placed throughout your institution. showcasing a number of services available for you to avail of when needed. This campaign will only run for three days but fortunately these services will remain open throughout the year. We would now ask each and everyone of you in attendance to stand and join in a minute's silence, to remember those whom we have unfortunately lost to these complex mental illnesses'. as we strive to promote the services that can and hopefully will help save lives, keeping in mind always...
